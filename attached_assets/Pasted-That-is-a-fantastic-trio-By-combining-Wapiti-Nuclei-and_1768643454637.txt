### The "Diversified Attack" Workflow

| Step | Tool | Goal | Why it's modern |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Recon** | **WAFW00F** | Identify if Cloudflare is active. | Prevents you from accidentally testing against a WAF that will ban you. |
| **2. Scan** | **Nuclei** | Check for 2026-era exploits and headers. | Uses "Templates" that are updated daily for the latest CVEs. |
| **3. Attack** | **Wapiti** | Perform fuzzing and "injection" attacks. | It actually tries to bypass your forms and login logic. |

---

### How to Run Them Together (The Shell Script)

You can create a simple bash script in your Replit shell (e.g., `audit.sh`) to run these in order. This ensures you don't overwhelm your server.

```bash
#!/bin/bash
TARGET="http://127.0.0.1:5001"

echo "--- 1. FINGERPRINTING WAF ---"
wafw00f $TARGET

echo "--- 2. RUNNING MODERN EXPLOIT SCAN (NUCLEI) ---"
# This checks for misconfigurations and technology-specific bugs
nuclei -u $TARGET -tags misconfig,tech,exposures

echo "--- 3. DYNAMIC FUZZING (WAPITI) ---"
# This crawls your site and tries to inject XSS/SQL payloads
wapiti -u $TARGET/ -o wapiti_report --flush-session

```

---

### Pro-Tips for Your Setup

1. **Avoid the "Ban":** When scanning your **live Cloudflare URL**, use the `-p` (proxy) or rate-limiting flags in Nuclei. Cloudflare's free tier has "Under Attack" mode which might trigger and block your Replit IP if you scan too fast.
2. **Authenticated Scans:** If your project has a login, use `wapiti-getcookie` first. This allows Wapiti to log in and scan the "hidden" parts of your site that only users can see.
3. **Clean Reports:** Since you are using **Cloudflare Pages**, keep an eye on the "Security Headers" section of your reports. If Nuclei still says headers are missing, it means you need to update that `_headers` file we talked about earlier.
